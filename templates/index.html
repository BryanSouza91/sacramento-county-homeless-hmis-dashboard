Sacramento Homeless Services Dashboard

Homelessness poses a major challenge to Sacramento. In an effort to better understand the issue, Code for Sacramento developed the following data visualizations of the volume of homeless-related services, how many people find permanent housing through these programs, and the demographics of those who use the services. The bulk of the data comes from Sacramento's Homeless Management Information System (HMIS), which is used by local homeless service providers to coordinate care, manage their operations, and better serve their clients. As of early 2020, the most recent data is available through Augst 2019. The raw data can be accessed at Code for Sacramento.

PROGRAM VOLUME

The 2019 Sacramento Homeless Point in Time (PIT) Count indicated that 5,570 persons experience homelessness on a given night, an increase of 19% countywide compared to 2017. A count is held every two years and was last conducted over two nights in January 2019 (Source). Note that not all who are homeless seek or receive services, and some services included below are designed to prevent homelessness.
